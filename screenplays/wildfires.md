# 🔥 WILDFIRES

## Segment Two of the Climate Anthology

---

**LOGLINE**: A smokejumper returns to the California Sierra for a season that will burn hotter and longer than any before — and discovers the fire has followed her home.

---

### WRITING PROMPTS & RESEARCH ANCHORS

- **Wildfire escalation**: A 2022 Nature study ("Global increase in wildfire potential from compound fire weather") found that upward trends in climate-related wildfire potential are driven by increasingly severe fire weather, reflecting the impacts of anthropogenic climate change.
- **Fire weather compounds**: Simultaneous heat, drought, and wind create compound conditions that make megafires more likely and harder to control.
- **Air quality cascade**: Wildfire smoke can travel thousands of miles. Dhaka's PM2.5 spikes (21–43 µg/m³) offer a template for describing what smoke does to a body — burning eyes, persistent cough, the taste of ash.
- **Fire season length**: IPCC AR6 reports that fire weather seasons have lengthened globally, with the burned area increasing in many regions.
- **Ecological tipping points**: Some forests may not regenerate after severe fires, converting to shrubland or grassland.
- **Human cost**: The UN reports climate change affects 3.6 billion people; wildfires destroy homes, livelihoods, and mental health.

### KEY THEMES
- The professionalization of trauma
- Nature as an unrecognizable force
- The smoke that doesn't stay "out there"
- Community under siege from the sky
- Climate refugees within wealthy nations

### SCREENPLAY

**FADE IN:**

**EXT. SIERRA NEVADA RIDGELINE — DUSK**

*The sky is the color of a bruise — purple and orange, streaked with smoke plumes that stretch to the horizon. Below, a forest of ponderosa pines. Or what used to be.*

*ELENA (30s), smoke-stained face, helmet in hand, stands at the edge of a fire break. Her radio crackles with frantic chatter she's stopped listening to.*

*Behind her, a SQUAD of smokejumpers sits in exhausted silence, their silver suits blackened.*

> **ELENA**
> (into radio)
> Base, this is Delta Squad. We've lost the ridge. Repeat — we've lost the ridge.

*Static. Then:*

> **BASE (V.O.)**
> Copy, Delta. Fall back to the river. All units fall back.

*Elena looks down at the valley where she grew up. A line of fire is crawling toward it like something alive.*
