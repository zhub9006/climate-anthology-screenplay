# 🌊 RISING SEAS

## Segment One of the Climate Anthology

---

**LOGLINE**: When the Buriganga river swallows the Old Dhaka waterfront, a family of boat builders must choose between the only home they've ever known and an uncertain future on higher ground.

---

### WRITING PROMPTS & RESEARCH ANCHORS

- **Sea-level rise projections**: IPCC AR6 projects 0.3–1.0 m of global mean sea-level rise by 2100 (SSP1-2.6 to SSP5-8.5). Under high emissions, some models project 2+ m.
- **Bangladesh vulnerability**: A 1 m rise could displace ~30 million people in Bangladesh. Dhaka sits at ~8 m elevation with millions in informal settlements along riverbanks.
- **Current Dhaka conditions**: 85% humidity, monsoon rains, PM2.5 spikes to 40+ µg/m³ at night. The air is thick and difficult to breathe.
- **Cyclone intensification**: IPCC AR6 WG1 Ch.11 confirms intensification of tropical cyclones with increased rainfall rates.
- **Saltwater intrusion**: Rising seas push salt water into the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta, destroying rice paddies and freshwater supplies.

### KEY THEMES
- Displacement vs. resilience
- Intergenerational knowledge (boat building) becoming obsolete
- The river as both lifeline and threat
- Urban flooding in a megacity of 22 million

### SCREENPLAY

**FADE IN:**

**EXT. BURIGANGA RIVER — DAWN**

*The river is a sheet of grey silk under a sky the color of old pewter. Small boats bob at their moorings. The waterline is higher than it should be — two feet up the stone steps of the ghat.*

*FARHANA (40s), calloused hands, weathered face, works on a wooden hull propped on the riverbank. Her daughter NISHAT (17) watches the water with an expression between reverence and dread.*

> **NISHAT**
> It's coming up again, Ma.

*Farhana doesn't look up from her plane.*

> **FARHANA**
> It always comes up in the monsoon.

> **NISHAT**
> It's not monsoon yet.

*Beat. Farhana stops planing. Looks at the water. Looks at her daughter.*
