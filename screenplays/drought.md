# ☀️ DROUGHT

## Segment Three of the Climate Anthology

---

**LOGLINE**: In a Sahel village where the rains haven't come in three years, a young hydrologist and an elder farmer clash over a dried riverbed — and discover the water never left; it just went deeper than anyone thought.

---

### WRITING PROMPTS & RESEARCH ANCHORS

- **Drought intensification**: IPCC AR6 confirms increases in agricultural and ecological drought across many regions. ScienceDirect (2023) identifies drought as one of the direct impacts of climate change alongside intensified severe weather events.
- **Heat extremes**: Dhaka's heat index reaching 41.7 °C (107 °F) is a reference point. In the Sahel, temperatures regularly exceed 45 °C during drought. At these levels, outdoor labor becomes fatal.
- **Water scarcity & conflict**: The UN reports that climate change causes dangerous disruption in nature, affecting billions. Water scarcity drives migration and conflict.
- **Agricultural collapse**: IPCC AR6 WGII documents crop yield declines of 10–25% in drought-prone regions under 2 °C warming.
- **Groundwater depletion**: Over-extraction of aquifers is accelerating. Some Sahel aquifers are fossil water — once depleted, they won't refill.
- **El Niño amplification**: NASA reports El Niño events are intensifying, exacerbating drought in some regions while causing flooding in others.
- **Dust and air quality**: Drought creates dust storms. Dhaka's PM10 values (26–49 µg/m³) are a baseline; during Sahel dust events, PM10 can exceed 1,000 µg/m³.

### KEY THEMES
- Traditional knowledge vs. scientific method
- The invisible underground resource
- Intergenerational guilt and hope
- Climate migration as survival, not defeat
- The body under heat stress

### SCREENPLAY

**FADE IN:**

**EXT. SAHEL VILLAGE — MIDDAY**

*The sun is a white hole in a bleached sky. The earth is cracked in geometric patterns — hexagons, pentagons, like a honeycomb of baked mud. A dead acacia tree stands at the village edge, its branches like charred bones.*

*AMINATA (20s), practical clothes, carries a ground-penetrating radar unit across the dry riverbed. She consults a tablet, frowns, adjusts a dial.*

*OLD MUSA (70s), thin but strong, watches from the shade of a mud-brick wall. He holds a calabash of water like it's the last one on earth.*

> **AMINATA**
> There's nothing here. The river hasn't flowed in three years.

> **OLD MUSA**
> The river is not gone. It is sleeping.

> **AMINATA**
> (checking tablet)
> My instruments say otherwise.

*Old Musa takes a long, slow sip of water.*

> **OLD MUSA**
> Your instruments do not listen.
